Voice Data Collection for Psychiatric Evaluation and Research

Speech is a fundamental means of communication for understanding and assessing mental health. This project aims to harness digital voice recordings and artificial intelligence to enhance the diagnosis and evaluation of psychiatric and neurological disorders in clinical outpatient settings.

Objectives of the project are as follows:

  • Development of AI-Based Evaluation Tools: Create and refine artificial intelligence methods for analyzing voice recordings to improve diagnostic accuracy in mental health care.
  • Application Development for Clinical Use: Build software solutions that assist clinicians in the interpretation and assessment of psychiatric symptoms using recorded patient interviews.
  • Early Detection and Improved Outcomes: Enable early identification of conditions such as Alzheimerâ€™s disease, schizophrenia, suicide risk, substance use disorders, major depressive disorder, autism spectrum disorder, and ADHD through advanced data analytics and machine learning.
  • Confidential and Ethical Data Collection: Collect voice data from consenting patients in strict confidence, ensuring privacy and voluntary participation at every stage.
